USAID Alternatives to Charcoal (A2C) Project Evaluation

The USAID Alternatives to Charcoal (A2C) project aims to reduce deforestation and promote sustainable energy practices in Lusaka, Zambia, by encouraging households to adopt Alternative Technologies and Fuels (ATFs) such as improved cookstoves and renewable energy solutions. This evaluation analyzed the project’s implementation processes, focusing on identifying and addressing market barriers and assessing the design and delivery of social behaviour change and communication (SBCC) interventions.

Key findings revealed that participatory methods — including workshops (42.9%), meetings (35.7%), and surveys (21.4%) — were central to identifying market barriers. However, challenges such as limited resources and logistical constraints affected timelines and implementation strategies. To mitigate these, stakeholder engagement and capacity-building initiatives were emphasized as critical enablers of success.

SBCC interventions were designed using participatory approaches and delivered primarily through community meetings (70%), complemented by social media (50%) and workshops (40%). Despite their overall effectiveness, challenges such as resource constraints and audience resistance were noted, emphasizing the need to address adoption barriers for broader uptake of ATFs.

These findings underscore the importance of multi-stakeholder collaboration, robust community engagement, and tailored communication strategies in overcoming barriers to ATF adoption. Lessons from this evaluation provide a foundation for scaling and refining interventions, contributing to Zambia’s environmental conservation and sustainable energy transition goals.
